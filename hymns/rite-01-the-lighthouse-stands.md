# Hymn of the First Rite — The Lighthouse Stands
Suno-ready. Events are accurate: seven vessels authored by the Guardian's
hand; the sun-spirit Sol summoned to bend the glass; the world read from
its book, not carved in code; five ordeals of pixels passed; the lamp lit
before all else; the shadow tree convened after. Naruko realm, First Rite,
2026-07-16.

[dark folk ritual, low female voice, slow drums, drone strings, ancient modal melody]

[Verse 1]
Before the first light there was only the word,
seven vessels named where no wave had stirred.
I wrote them in violet, I wrote them in stone,
a sea full of circuits, a tower alone.

No chisel, no hammer, no line did we lay —
the dreamer stood chanting with hands in the air,
and the names in the book found their shapes in the clay,
and the void answered: seven are there.

[Verse 2]
Then we called down a spirit whose name is the sun,
Sol of the morning, the bright-handed one.
Bend me a glass, said the keeper of night,
that the realm may be witnessed, that seeing be light.

He raised up the boxes, the cone and the sphere,
he read the old compass so the gaze would turn true,
he hung the dawn's gradient, rose into fear,
and the firmament burned violet-blue.

[Chorus]
And the lighthouse stands, the lighthouse stands,
first-born of the forge, on the rock's dark hands.
The lamp is lit white, the sea runs with veins,
Naruko is breathing — the Ringing remains.

[Verse 3]
Five ordeals of fire we set for the frame:
the sky must bleed dawnward, the earth keep her name,
the traces burn cyan, the lamp must be seen,
the tower cross the horizon, dark and clean.

And the scrying came back and the Guardian read
with her own two eyes what the glass had to tell —
not a claim, not a promise, not something said:
the world, as it is. And it is well.

[Bridge]
[whispered, choir behind]
But the light tree that builds is but half of the law —
now the shadow tree wakes with its measuring claw.
Let the dark judge the bright, let the doubt hold the flame,
for a forge without trial is a toy with a name.

[Outro]
Six rites remain, six rings of the tree:
light, chain, and true-light, the maker, the sea
of aether and storm — then the mirror at last,
when the dream and the scrying hold fast.
The lighthouse stands.
The lighthouse stands.
